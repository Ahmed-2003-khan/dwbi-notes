1. Quality ka Matlab

Formal Definitions:

  • P. Crosby (1979): “Quality is conformance to requirements” → matlab cheez customer ki expectations ke mutabiq honi chahiye.
  • Webster Dictionary: “Degree of excellence” → kisi cheez ka kitna excellent ya superior hona.

Auto Industry Example

  • Quality ka matlab sirf exceeding expectations nahi, balki customer ki real needs meet karna hai.
  • Quality me wo improvements shamil hain jo customer ki life easy aur comfortable banate hain.
  • Auto industry ka example isliye: yahan quality improvements ka effect turant nazar aata hai aur measurable hota hai.
Slide me diagram

Slide me ek car aur uske features ka diagram tha jahan highlight kiya gaya ke quality ka focus customer ki needs par hai.

2. Data kya hai?

Har data kisi real-world entity ka abstraction hota hai.

3. Data Quality kya hai?

  • Intrinsic Data Quality: Data ka correctness ya accuracy.
  • Realistic Data Quality: Data ka business ke liye utility/value.

Example: Agar employee ka height galat record hai → intrinsic quality low. Agar weight aur age HR decisions me kaam aa rahi hai → realistic quality high.

4. Data Quality & Organizations

  • Intelligent Learning Organization: High-quality data shared resource hai aur value-add processes hain.
  • Functional Learning Organization: Low-quality data proprietary hai aur cost-add processes hain.

5. Orr’s Laws of Data Quality

  1. Unused data cannot be correct.
  2. Data quality = function of use, not collection.
  3. Data quality limited by most stringent use.
  4. Data quality problems increase with system age.
  5. Rare events are most damaging.

6. Total Quality Management (TQM)

  • Systematic aur continuous improvement ka philosophy.
  • Customer-oriented: Har improvement customer value increase kare.
  • Includes: Product quality, process control, quality assurance, quality improvement.
  • QA ≠ Quality Improvement

7. Cost of Fixing Data Quality

Defect minimize karna economical, defect eliminate karna expensive.

8. Cost of Data Quality Defects

  • Controllable Costs: analysis, correction, prevention
  • Resultant Costs: missed opportunities, failures
  • Equipment & Training Costs

9. Where Data Quality is Critical?

  • Marketing communications
  • Customer matching
  • Retail house-holding
  • MIS integration after acquisition

10. Data Quality Characteristics

  • Accuracy
  • Completeness
  • Believability
  • Appropriate Amount
  • Timeliness
  • Accessibility
  • Objectivity
  • Interpretability

Completeness vs Accuracy Example

95% accurate & 100% complete OR 100% accurate & 95% complete → application, tolerance, cost, business value par depend karta hai.

11. Data Quality Assessment Techniques

  • Simple Ratios: Free-of-error, completeness, consistency
  • Min-Max Technique: multiple values aggregation
  • Believability: compare with standard/experience
  • Timeliness: Max{0,1-C/V}
  • Accessibility: Max{0,1-Trd/Tru}

12. Data Quality Validation Techniques

  • Referential Integrity (RI)
  • Attribute Domain Validation
  • Data Quality Rules
  • Histogram Validation

Referential Integrity Example

Outstanding payments without customer_ID → orphan records. Weekly/monthly check in DWH.

Business vs Performance Case

  • Business: actual amount per year/region
  • Performance: enforcing RI costly, constraints off/discard violating records

13. Attribute Domain Validation

  1. Step 1: Count occurrences of each domain value.
  2. Step 2: Compare actual vs valid values.
  3. Step 3: Investigate exceptions (cause + impact).

14. Statistical Validation – Histogram

15. Data Quality Management (TDQM) Process

  1. Establish DQ Environment: IS managers, developers, professionals
  2. Scope Projects & Implementation Plan: goals, baseline, schedule, resources
  3. Implement Projects: Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve
  4. Evaluate Methods: assess improvement, modify, adopt as new business approach

17. Misconceptions

  • Data fixable independently → galat
  • DQ = IT problem → galat, company-wide responsibility
  • Cleansing tools ≠ complete solution
  • Quality vs ROI → joint business & IT effort

18. GIGO

Garbage In, Garbage Out → input galat hoga to output bhi galat hoga.
