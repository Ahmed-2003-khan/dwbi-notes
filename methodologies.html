1. DWH vs. Traditional IS

Key point: DWH project ko aam Information Systems se zyada scientific research jaisa samjho — requirements shuru mein clear nahin hoti aur bahut experiment/iteration chahiye hoti hai.

  • Scientific nature: Exploratory kaam — data exploration, modelling, aur hypothesis testing jaisa workflow.
  • Implication: Plan flexible rakho — early requirements par full reliance khatarnaak ho sakta hai.

2. Implementation Strategies

DWH build karne ke do aam approaches — dono ke pros/cons samajhna zaroori hai.

  1. Top-Down Approach
    Enterprise-level warehouse design pehle — phir data marts. Strong central design, consistency achi rehti hai lekin initial cost aur time zyada.
  2. Bottom-Up Approach
    Chhote data marts banate hue integrate karte hain. Jaldi value milti hai, par integration aur global consistency after mein manage karni parti hai.

3. DWH & Software Engineering Models

DWH development me software-engineering kaafi models use hote hain — har model ki fit-alignment project ki clarity, risk aur time par depend karti hai.

Waterfall Model

  • Sequential phases: Requirements → Design → Implementation → Testing → Deployment → Maintenance.
  • Best jab requirements stable hon.
  • Exam tip: Yad rakho ke waterfall me feedback loop limited hota hai.
[Diagram: Waterfall — sequential boxes stacked]

Spiral Model

  • Actually multiple small waterfall cycles — har cycle me planning, risk analysis, engineering, evaluation.
  • Risk-oriented aur iterative — unclear requirements ke liye suitable.
  • Exam tip: Spiral ko "risk-driven" model kehte hain kyunki har iteration risk ko identify aur reduce karta hai.
[Diagram: Spiral — iterations ka circular flow]

RAD & Agile

  • Iterative, prototyping-focused. Phases: scope → analyze → design → construct → test → review.
  • Automated tools aur rapid feedback cycles se development fast hoti hai.
  • Exam tip: RAD prototypes user ki expectations jaldi clarify kar dete hain — lekin production-quality alag kaam hai.
[Diagram: Iterative loops with prototype deliveries]

4. 5 Keys to Successful Prototyping

Prototyping DWH projects me bohat important hai — exam me in 5 points pooche ja sakte hain.

  1. Behtareen Team: DB programmers, designers, QA, decision-support specialists — lead by single manager. (Why? coordination simple rahega.)
  2. Focus Group: Novice + experienced users aur managers ka chhota group — continuous feedback deta rahega.
  3. User Manual First: Early UI + manual banane se users requirements clearly bata dete hain.
  4. Tools: Rapid-prototyping specific tools use karo — speed aur iteration main madad milegi.
  5. Prototype ≠ Final: Prototype sirf requirement discovery aur vision alignment ka tool hai — final app alag process se banega.

6. Project Start — Kahan se shuru karen?

Shuruwat se pehle ye sawalat clear karo — exam me inka zikr aksar hota hai.

  • Problems: DWH kaunse specific problem(s) solve karega? Problems precise, clear, testable honi chahiye.
  • Success Criteria: Kaise measure karenge? (e.g., 20% growth ya decision time reduce hona).
  • Time & Money (Scope): Budget aur schedule kaise manage karenge?
  • Skills: Kaunse technical aur domain skills required hain?

7. DWH Lifecycle Model (General)

Typical iterative lifecycle — exam me order yad rakhna important hai.

  1. Design — Architecture, models, scope.
  2. Prototype — Rapid implementation of core features for feedback.
  3. Deploy — Production rollout.
  4. Operate — Daily use, monitoring, support.
  5. Enhance — New features, scale, performance improvements.

8. Kimball’s Business Dimensional Lifecycle (Goal Driven)

Kimball ka approach business-oriented hai — project management strong hona chahiye.

1. Project Planning

  • Readiness check: sponsor, business motivation, feasibility, scope, culture.
  • Justify: cost vs benefit (ROI estimate).
  • Build team: strong PM + business partnership.

2. Business Requirement Definition

  • Requirement collection: documentation, interviews, observation, questionnaires.
  • Post-collection: Prioritization (what to build first).
  • Quadrant technique: prioritize by Business Impact vs Feasibility.

3. Design & Development Tracks (Parallel)

  • Technology track: Architecture design, product selection, infra.
  • Data track: Dimensional modelling, physical design, ETL (data staging).
  • Application track: Analytic app specs, BI/report dev.

4. Deployment

Production launch — performance testing, go-live plan, rollback strategy.

5. Maintenance & Growth

Continuous monitoring, adding data sources, scaling, feature enhancement.

9. Requirements Gathering & Prioritization

  • Methods: Documentation review, interviews, observation, research, questionnaires.
  • Quadrant Technique: Requirements ko chart par rakh kar prioritize karo — High Impact / High Feasibility pe pehle kaam karo.
  • Exam hint: Quadrant = simple 2x2 matrix (Impact vs Feasibility) — short aur common question.
Quick Revision — Short Exam Notes
  • DWH = research-like: requirements ambiguous => iterate.
  • Top-down: enterprise first; Bottom-up: marts first.
  • Models: Waterfall (sequential), Spiral (risk-driven), RAD/Agile (iterative).
  • Prototyping keys: good team, focus group, UI/manual first, tools, prototype not final.
  • Methodologies: Inmon (data-driven), Kimball (goal-driven), Westerman (user-driven).
  • Kimball steps: Plan → Requirements → Design/ETL/App → Deploy → Maintain.
